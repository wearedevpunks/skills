# Intake Phase

Collect existing product truth before grilling or making new design work.

## Steps

1. Read available routes, pages, flows, and target surfaces.
2. Read theme CSS, tokens, global styles, and reusable component files.
3. Read screenshots when present or cheap to capture.
4. Read Figma/design inputs when supplied or connector-accessible.
5. Read content sources, copy constraints, brand constraints, and known design decisions.
6. Record missing Figma/screenshots as evidence gaps; do not block by default.
7. Classify confidence.

## Low-Confidence Rule

If intake has fewer than routes plus one style, component, or content source, ask one concrete clarification or request one screenshot. Do not invent a blank-slate design system silently.

## Output

- Scope units: user-visible pages or flows.
- Evidence summary: routes, styles, components, screenshots, design inputs, content, brand constraints.
- Confidence: high | medium | low-confidence.
- Staleness risks.
- Phase handoff with next route: grill when constraints are missing; prototype when constraints and artifact intent are already fresh.
